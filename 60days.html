60-Day Video Content Creation Plan

Total Content Creation

Total Videos: 60 videos

Total Duration: 1,200 minutes (or 20 hours)

Possible Achievements

Comprehensive Course:

  • Full Course Coverage: You can create a comprehensive course on Palo Alto Networks training, covering all major topics in-depth.
  • Structured Learning Path: Organize the videos into a well-structured learning path, such as beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels.

Multiple Modules:

  • Separate Modules: Each video can cover different modules or chapters, ensuring each topic is thoroughly explained.
  • Detailed Walkthroughs: Provide detailed walkthroughs and hands-on labs to reinforce learning.

Supplementary Material:

  • Q&A Sessions: Include videos dedicated to answering common questions or doubts.
  • Practical Examples: Demonstrate practical examples and real-world applications of Palo Alto Networks configurations and management.

Certification Preparation:

  • Exam Focused Content: Develop specific videos aimed at helping students prepare for Palo Alto Networks certifications (PCNSA, PCNSE, PCDRA).
  • Mock Tests and Reviews: Include mock tests, reviews of sample questions, and strategies for tackling certification exams.

Community Engagement:

  • Live Sessions: Schedule live Q&A or interactive sessions periodically to engage with your students and build a community.
  • Feedback Incorporation: Use student feedback to adjust the content and address specific learning needs.

Content Distribution

Online Platforms:

  • YouTube: Regularly upload the videos to your YouTube channel to build an audience.
  • Udemy/Coursera: Publish the complete course on platforms like Udemy or Coursera for a wider reach.

Website:

  • Course Portal: Create a dedicated course portal on your website where students can access the videos, supplementary materials, and quizzes.

Marketing:

  • Social Media: Promote the videos and course content through social media channels to attract potential students.
  • Email Marketing: Use email marketing to notify your subscribers about new video releases and course updates.

Example Breakdown

  • Week 1-2: Introduction to Palo Alto Networks, basic concepts, and initial setup.
  • Week 3-4: Intermediate topics, firewall configurations, and network security policies.
  • Week 5-6: Advanced topics, threat prevention, and management.
  • Week 7-8: Hands-on labs, practical scenarios, and real-world applications.
  • Week 9-10: Exam preparation, mock tests, and review sessions.

Conclusion

By consistently recording 20-minute videos every day for 60 days, you can create a comprehensive and structured training program that covers all essential aspects of Palo Alto Networks. This will not only provide valuable content to your audience but also establish you as a leading trainer in the industry.
